<p><p>Intramedullary nails are medical implants used in orthopedic surgeries to treat bone fractures. These nails are inserted into the medullary canal (the hollow space inside the bone) to stabilize the fracture and promote proper healing. They are typically made of stainless steel or titanium and are available in different sizes and designs to suit various types of fractures.</p><p>The current outlook of the intramedullary nails market is positive and expected to show steady growth in the coming years. The market is primarily driven by factors such as the increasing incidence of bone fractures, the rise in road accidents, and a growing elderly population susceptible to fractures. Additionally, advancements in nail technology, such as the development of bioresorbable nails and minimally invasive surgical techniques, are further propelling the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is benefiting from the increasing adoption of intramedullary nails in developing regions, as healthcare infrastructure and surgical expertise improve.
