<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Nonwovens for hygiene market comprises three major types: spunmelt, spunbonded, and spunlace nonwovens. Spunmelt nonwovens are produced by combining spunbond and meltblown technologies, resulting in a fabric with excellent barrier properties and absorbent qualities. Spunbonded nonwovens, on the other hand, are made by extruding continuous filaments that are subsequently bonded together. This type of nonwoven offers strength, durability, and excellent uniformity. Lastly, spunlace nonwovens are created by entangling fibers using high-pressure water jets, resulting in a soft and strong fabric with high absorbency.</p></p>
