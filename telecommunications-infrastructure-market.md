<p><p>The telecommunications infrastructure market is composed of various types of towers used for wireless communication. Lattice towers are large structures consisting of interconnected metal beams, offering great strength and versatility. Guyed towers feature guy wires that provide stability, commonly used in locations with high wind conditions. Monopole towers are single-pole structures, suitable for urban areas due to their aesthetic appeal. Stealth towers are designed to be visually disguised as trees or other structures. Lastly, the "Others" category encompasses different types of towers specific to unique scenarios, such as chimney towers or rooftop installations.</p></p>
