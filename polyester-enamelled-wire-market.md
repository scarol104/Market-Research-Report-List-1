THEIC Modified Enamelled Wire is a type of polyester enamelled wire that is modified using THEIC (Tri(2,3-epoxypropyl)isocyanurate), a type of resin. This modification enhances the wire's electrical and mechanical properties, making it suitable for applications requiring high temperature and resistance to chemicals. Imine Modified Enamelled Wire, on the other hand, is a polyester enamelled wire modified with imine, which improves its thermal and electrical conductivity. Both types cater to specific requirements in various industries.</p></p>
