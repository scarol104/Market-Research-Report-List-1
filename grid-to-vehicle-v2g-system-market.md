<p><p>The Grid-to-Vehicle (V2G) System market can be categorized into three types: centralized, autonomous, and micronet-based. In a centralized market, a third-party system operator acts as an intermediary between electric vehicle (EV) owners and the power grid. An autonomous market allows direct communication between individual EVs and the grid, enabling them to buy and sell electricity independently. In a micronet-based market, localized EV clusters form their own microgrid, enabling peer-to-peer energy trading within the group. These market types describe different approaches to integrating EVs with the power grid for efficient energy management.</p></p>
