<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The vegetarian capsule materials market comprises three main types: Hydroxypropyl Methyl Cellulose (HPMC), Pullulan, and Starch. HPMC capsules, made from natural plant cellulose, are widely used due to their high stability, moisture retention, and compatibility with a variety of drugs. Pullulan capsules, derived from fermented starch, offer excellent oxygen barrier properties, making them suitable for oxygen-sensitive drugs. Starch capsules, made from corn or potato starch, are gaining popularity as they are biodegradable and offer good mechanical properties. These materials provide alternative options for vegetarians or those with dietary restrictions.</p></p>
