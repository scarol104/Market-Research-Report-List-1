<p><p>Water scale inhibitors and dispersants are chemicals used to control and prevent the formation of scale deposits in water systems. Inorganic type inhibitors and dispersants are made up of minerals and compounds such as phosphates and phosphonates, which act by interfering with the crystal growth of scale-forming minerals. On the other hand, organic type inhibitors and dispersants consist of organic polymers that work by adsorbing onto the surface of scale-forming minerals, preventing them from adhering to the system's surfaces. Both types of inhibitors and dispersants are essential for maintaining the efficiency and longevity of water systems.</p></p>
