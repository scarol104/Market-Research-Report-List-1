<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The reactive emulsifier market is categorized into two types: anionic emulsifier and nonionic reactive emulsifier. Anionic emulsifiers are negatively charged surface-active agents that provide stability to emulsions by creating a repulsive force between droplets. Nonionic reactive emulsifiers, on the other hand, do not have a charge and are less sensitive to changes in pH or electrolyte concentration. Both types play a crucial role in the emulsification process, ensuring uniform dispersion of oil and water phases in various applications such as cosmetics, food processing, and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
