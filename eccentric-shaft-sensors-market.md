<p><p>Eccentric shaft sensors are used to detect the position and speed of the eccentric shaft in an engine. The market for these sensors can be classified into two types - OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) and Aftermarket.</p><p>OEM market refers to sensors that are manufactured by the same company that produces the engine or vehicle. These sensors are specifically designed to meet the specifications and requirements of the manufacturer. They are usually installed during the production process and are typically of high quality and reliability.</p><p>Aftermarket market, on the other hand, involves sensors that are produced by third-party manufacturers and are not installed during the initial manufacturing of the engine or vehicle. These sensors are designed to be compatible with a range of engines and vehicles, allowing vehicle owners to replace faulty or outdated sensors. Aftermarket sensors may vary in terms of quality and price, offering customers more options and flexibility.</p><p>Both OEM and aftermarket eccentric shaft sensors play a crucial role in maintaining the performance and efficiency of engines, but the key distinction lies in their origin and distribution.</p></p>
