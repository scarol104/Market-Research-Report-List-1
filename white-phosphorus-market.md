<p><p>White phosphorus is a highly reactive, colorless to yellowish waxy solid that consists of a P4 tetrahedron structure. It is commonly obtained from phosphate rocks and is primarily used in a variety of industrial applications. It has attributes such as extreme flammability, high reactivity, and is notably toxic. Due to these characteristics, white phosphorus is widely used as a reducing agent, smoke-screening agent, and in the production of pesticides, fireworks, and munitions.</p><p>The future outlook of the white phosphorus market seems promising.
