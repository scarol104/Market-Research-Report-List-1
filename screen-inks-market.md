<p><p>Screen inks are a type of ink used in screen printing, a technique that involves pressing ink through a mesh screen onto a substrate to create designs or images. Solvent-based screen inks use a solvent as the carrier to dissolve the ink pigments. They provide excellent durability and resistance on various substrates but can release harmful volatile organic compounds. Water-based screen inks, on the other hand, use water as the carrier. They have a lower environmental impact, are safer to handle, but may have lower durability compared to solvent-based inks.</p></p>
