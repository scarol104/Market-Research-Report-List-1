The increasing demand for animal-free and eco-friendly products, coupled with the rising concern for animal rights and environmental sustainability, is driving the market growth. The versatility and cost-effectiveness of PU synthetic leather compared to genuine leather are also contributing factors.</p><p>In addition, the growing fashion industry, along with rising disposable incomes and changing consumer preferences, is expected to further boost the demand for polyurethane-based synthetic leather products. <p><p>The polyurethane (PU)-based synthetic leather market can be categorized into three types. The first type is ordinary PU synthetic leather, which is made from PU resin and various additives. It is widely used in the automotive, furniture, and fashion industries. The second type is microfiber synthetic leather, which is created by blending microfiber and PU resin. It mimics the appearance and texture of genuine leather and is often utilized in the production of footwear and handbags. The third type encompasses other varieties of synthetic leather that do not fit into the above two categories, such as PU-coated fabrics and PVC-based synthetic leathers.</p></p>
