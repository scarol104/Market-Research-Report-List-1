<p><p>Bauxite aggregate market types are determined by the presence of various compounds. Aluminum Oxide (Al2O3) is the main constituent and defines the aluminum content. Iron Oxide (Fe2O3) influences the color and durability of the aggregate. Titanium Dioxide (TiO2) affects the aggregate's resistance to weathering. Calcium Oxide (CaO) and Magnesium Oxide (MgO) contribute to the overall chemical composition of the aggregate. Potassium Oxide (K2O) and Sodium Oxide (Na2O) influence the aggregate's reactivity and can affect its suitability for certain applications. These compound variations offer different characteristics and applications within the bauxite aggregate market.</p></p>
