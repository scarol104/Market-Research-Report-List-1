<p><p>Normal Alpha Olefins (NAO) are a type of olefins with a linear structure and are commonly used in various applications. The market for NAO can be segmented into different types based on the length of the carbon chain. Butene-1 is a four-carbon chain olefin used in the production of polymers, solvents, and synthetic lubricants. Hexene-1, with a six-carbon chain, is mainly utilized in the manufacturing of high-density polyethylene (HDPE). Octene-1, an eight-carbon chain olefin, is commonly used in the production of linear low-density polyethylene (LLDPE) and as a comonomer in the production of polyethylene. Other NAOs include higher carbon chain length olefins used in diverse applications such as plasticizers, surfactants, and lubricants.</p></p>
