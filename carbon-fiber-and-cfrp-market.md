<p><p>Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer (CFRP) is a composite material that combines carbon fibers and a polymer matrix, resulting in a lightweight, strong, and high-performance material. The CFRP market can be categorized into two types: thermosetting CFRP and thermoplastic CFRP. Thermosetting CFRP involves the carbon fibers being impregnated with a thermosetting resin and then cured through heat or chemical reaction, providing excellent mechanical properties. On the other hand, thermoplastic CFRP utilizes a thermoplastic polymer matrix that can be melted and reprocessed multiple times, offering flexibility and recyclability while maintaining good strength and stiffness.</p></p>
