<p><p>Electronic Fetal Monitoring (EFM) is a medical technique used to monitor the fetal heart rate and maternal uterine contractions during pregnancy and labor. EFM allows healthcare providers to assess the well-being of the fetus and detect any signs of distress or abnormality in real-time. It uses devices such as electronic transducers and ultrasound dopplers to record and display the fetal heart rate and contractions.</p><p>The global Electronic Fetal Monitoring (EFM) market is expected to experience significant growth during the forecast period. The market is driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of high-risk pregnancies, rising awareness about prenatal care, and technological advancements in fetal monitoring devices.</p><p>One of the major trends observed in the EFM market is the shift from traditional invasive methods to non-invasive wireless monitoring systems. Wireless monitoring systems provide greater comfort and mobility to pregnant women during labor and allow healthcare providers to access real-time data remotely. This trend is expected to fuel market growth as it improves patient experience and enables better healthcare outcomes.</p><p>Another trend in the EFM market is the adoption of portable and handheld monitoring devices. These devices offer convenience and ease of use, enhancing the ability of healthcare professionals to monitor fetal health efficiently. Portability and miniaturization of monitoring devices enable continuous monitoring and promote early detection of fetal distress.</p><p>The increasing focus on personalized medicine is also anticipated to drive market growth.
