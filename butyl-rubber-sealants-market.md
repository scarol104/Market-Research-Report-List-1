<p><ul><li>Vulcanized Type</li><li>Non-Vulcanized Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Butyl rubber sealants are widely used in various industries for their excellent sealing properties. There are two main types of butyl rubber sealants: vulcanized type and non-vulcanized type. </p><p>Vulcanized type sealants undergo a process called vulcanization, which involves the addition of sulfur to make the rubber more durable and resistant to wear and tear. This type of sealant is commonly used in applications that require high strength and long-term durability, such as automotive, construction, and aerospace.</p><p>On the other hand, non-vulcanized type sealants do not go through the vulcanization process. They are more pliable and easier to apply, making them suitable for applications that require flexibility and easy installation, such as DIY projects or temporary fixes.</p><p>Both types of butyl rubber sealants offer excellent weather resistance, water resistance, and adhesion properties, providing reliable sealing solutions for a wide range of applications.</p></p>
