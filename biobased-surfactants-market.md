<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Biobased surfactants are categorized into four main types: anionic, cationic, nonionic, and amphoteric. Anionic surfactants possess a negative charge and are commonly used in detergents and cleaning products. Cationic surfactants have a positive charge and are mainly utilized in personal care products and fabric softeners. Nonionic surfactants have no charge and are versatile, employed in a wide range of applications such as cosmetics and industrial cleaning. Lastly, amphoteric surfactants can carry both positive and negative charges, making them effective in a variety of products including shampoos and conditioners.</p></p>
