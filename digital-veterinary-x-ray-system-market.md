<p><p>The digital veterinary X-ray system market consists of two main types: Computed Radiography (CR) and Direct Radiography (DR). Computed Radiography utilizes special imaging plates to capture X-ray images, which are then converted into a digital format for viewing. On the other hand, Direct Radiography directly captures and converts X-ray images into digital form, eliminating the need for imaging plates. Both technologies offer the advantages of quick image acquisition, ease of manipulation, and enhanced diagnostic capabilities in veterinary practices.</p></p>
