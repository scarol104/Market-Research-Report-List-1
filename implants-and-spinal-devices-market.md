<p><p>Implants and spinal devices are medical devices used to treat various spinal disorders and injuries. This growth is primarily attributed to factors such as an increasing prevalence of spinal disorders and injuries, growing geriatric population, advancements in surgical techniques, and rising healthcare expenditure.</p><p>The market is also driven by technological advancements in implant materials, such as titanium and biocompatible polymers, which provide better strength, durability, and biocompatibility. Additionally, the advent of minimally invasive surgical techniques has further boosted the demand for implants and spinal devices, as they offer faster recovery, reduced pain, and reduced hospital stays.</p><p>Furthermore, the aging population and the associated age-related spinal disorders, such as degenerative disc disease and osteoporosis, are expected to drive the demand for implants and spinal devices.
