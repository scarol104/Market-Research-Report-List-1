<p><p>Glass cenospheres are versatile lightweight hollow spheres used in various industries. They are categorized into two types: floating beads and sinking beads. Floating beads have a lower density, enabling them to float in liquids, while sinking beads have a higher density, causing them to sink. Floating beads are commonly employed in insulation materials, lightweight fillers, and oil drilling, as they enhance buoyancy and thermal insulation. Conversely, sinking beads find applications in paints, coatings, and composites, as they improve strength, durability, and conductivity. These market types cater to different industrial needs, allowing for diverse applications of glass cenospheres.</p></p>
