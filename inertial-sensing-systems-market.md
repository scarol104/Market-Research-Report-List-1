<p><p>Inertial sensing systems consist of Attitude Heading and Reference Systems (AHRS), Inertial Navigation Systems (INSs), and Inertial Measurement Units (IMUs). AHRS provides information about an object's orientation and angular velocity. INSs use accelerometers and gyroscopes to calculate an object's position, velocity, and orientation. IMUs combine accelerometer and gyroscope data to measure an object's linear acceleration and angular velocity. These sensing systems are widely used in various applications including aerospace, defense, automotive, and robotics industries for navigation, stability control, motion tracking, and virtual reality applications.</p></p>
