<p><p>Olefinic thermoplastic elastomers refer to a group of materials that possess both thermoplastic and elastomeric properties. Two common types of olefinic thermoplastic elastomers are thermoplastic polyolefins (TPOs) and thermoplastic vulcanizates (TPVs). TPOs are a blend of polypropylene and EPDM rubber, offering high heat resistance and flexibility. TPVs are a combination of a hard plastic matrix, such as polypropylene, and a crosslinked rubber phase, providing excellent chemical resistance and durability. Both TPOs and TPVs find applications in various industries, including automotive, construction, and consumer goods.</p></p>
