<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Automotive active seat head rests consist of two types: headrest activation and seat back activation. Headrest activation refers to the movement of the headrest itself in response to a specific triggering event, such as a rear impact, to provide better support and reduce the risk of whiplash injuries. Seat back activation, on the other hand, involves the entire seat back adjusting its position, along with the headrest, to provide additional protection and comfort for the occupant. Both types aim to enhance safety and improve the overall seating experience in vehicles.</p></p>
