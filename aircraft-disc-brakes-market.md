<p><p>Aircraft disc brakes are the braking systems used in aircraft to reduce the speed or stop the rotation of aircraft wheels during landing or during taxiing. These brakes consist of a cast-iron rotor and caliper assembly that squeeze the rotor to create the required braking torque. The rotor transfers the heat generated during braking to the atmosphere, preventing the brakes from overheating.</p><p>The aircraft disc brakes market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. Factors driving this growth include the increasing demand for new-generation aircraft with advanced braking systems, the rise in air passenger traffic worldwide, and the need for efficient braking systems to ensure safe landings. Additionally, the technological advancements in aircraft disc brakes, such as the development of lightweight and ceramic disc brakes, are also contributing to market growth.</p><p>In terms of market analysis, the global aircraft disc brakes market has been segmented based on aircraft type, component, end user, and region.
