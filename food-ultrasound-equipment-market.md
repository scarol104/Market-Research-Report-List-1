<p><p>High-frequency low-intensity food ultrasound equipment refers to devices that use high-frequency sound waves (typically above 1 MHz) with low power intensity to inspect and assess food quality, such as detecting defects or contamination. These systems provide detailed imaging and can be used in real-time, non-invasive applications. On the other hand, low-frequency high-intensity food ultrasound equipment uses lower frequency sound waves (typically below 1 MHz) with higher power intensity for various food processing applications, such as extracting compounds, improving texture, or accelerating certain processes like degassing or drying.</p></p>
