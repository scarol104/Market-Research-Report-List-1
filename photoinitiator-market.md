<p><p>Photoinitiators are substances widely used in the field of photopolymerization to facilitate the curing or hardening of various materials such as inks, coatings, adhesives, and plastics. There are two major types of photoinitiators: free-radical type and cationic type. Free-radical type photoinitiators initiates the polymerization process by generating free radicals upon exposure to light, whereas cationic type photoinitiators generate acid molecules that initiate the curing process. Both types play a vital role in enabling rapid and efficient curing of light-sensitive materials, improving their performance and durability.</p></p>
