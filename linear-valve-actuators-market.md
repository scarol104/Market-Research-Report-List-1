<p><p>Linear valve actuators are devices used to control the opening and closing of linear valves. There are two main types: diaphragm and piston actuators. Diaphragm actuators work by using a flexible diaphragm to convert pneumatic or hydraulic pressure into linear motion, thereby opening or closing the valve. Piston actuators, on the other hand, use a piston in a cylinder to generate linear force, resulting in valve operation. Both types find application in various industries where linear valve control is required, such as oil and gas, water treatment, and chemical processing.</p></p>
