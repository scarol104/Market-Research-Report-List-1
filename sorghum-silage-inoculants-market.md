<p><p>Sorghum silage inoculants are used to enhance the fermentation process in silage production. These inoculants can be classified into two types: heterofermentative and homofermentative. Heterofermentative inoculants contain a mix of different bacteria strains that produce a variety of fermentation by-products, resulting in improved preservation and reduced spoilage of sorghum silage. On the other hand, homofermentative inoculants consist of a single strain of bacteria that mainly produce lactic acid, leading to a faster and more efficient fermentation process. These types of inoculants offer different benefits in terms of silage quality and preservation.</p></p>
